# Activity Scoring

This document explains how each beach activity is scored and what
conditions are considered ideal, acceptable, or dangerous.

## Scoring System

Each activity receives a score from **0 to 100**:

| Score | Rating | Meaning |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 80–100 | Excellent | Perfect conditions — go now! |
| 60–79 | Good | Enjoyable conditions |
| 40–59 | Fair | Possible but not ideal |
| 20–39 | Poor | Conditions are challenging |
| 0–19 | Unsafe | Do not attempt this activity |

Each score starts at a baseline (50 for most, 60 for swimming) and
is adjusted up or down based on current conditions.

## Beach-Specific Modifiers

### Reef-Protected Beaches
**Diani, Watamu, Shanzu** have coral reef barriers that significantly
reduce wave energy in the lagoon:
- Swimming effective wave height = actual × 0.4
- Kids & dogs effective wave height = actual × 0.3
- These beaches score higher for swimming/kids even when offshore waves are large
- Surfing scores *lower* because the reef blocks rideable waves

### Current-Risk Beaches
**Kilifi, Lamu, Malindi** can experience strong tidal currents:
- Swimming and kids scores receive safety penalties
- Additional warnings are shown

### Coastline Orientation
The Kenyan coast primarily faces **east**. Wind direction relative to
the coastline determines:
- **Offshore wind** (from west/land): Good for surfing (clean wave faces)
- **Onshore wind** (from east/sea): Choppy conditions
- **Cross-shore wind** (from north/south): Best for kite surfing (safety)

## Surfing

### Ideal Conditions
- Wave height: **1.0–2.5 m**
- Wave period: **> 10 seconds** (long period = clean waves)
- Wind: **< 15 km/h offshore** (from the west)
- Tide: **rising** (mid-tide)
- No rain

### Scoring Breakdown

| Factor | Points | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Waves 1.0–2.5m | +25 | Ideal range |
| Waves 0.5–1.0m | +5 | Small — longboard needed |
| Waves 2.5–3.5m | +10 | Big — experts only |
| Waves > 3.5m | -20 | Dangerous |
| Waves < 0.5m | -25 | Flat — no surf |
| Period > 10s | +10 | Clean swell |
| Period 7–10s | +5 | Acceptable |
| Period < 7s | -5 | Choppy |
| Light offshore wind | +15 | Clean faces |
| Light any wind | +10 | Decent |
| Onshore wind | -10 | Choppy |
| Strong wind > 30 km/h | -10 | Difficult |
| Rising tide | +5 | Usually better |
| Reef-protected beach | -10 | Waves break on reef |

### Best Surf Beaches
1. **Malindi** — River mouth creates a sandbar break
2. **Mombasa** — Reef passages allow swell through
3. **Lamu** — Exposed to Indian Ocean swell

## Kite Surfing

### Ideal Conditions
- Wind: **20–35 km/h (11–19 knots)**
- Gust factor: **< 1.3** (steady wind)
- Waves: **< 1.5 m** (flat water preferred)
- Wind direction: **cross-shore** (30–120° from coast facing)

### Scoring Breakdown

| Factor | Points | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Wind 20–35 km/h | +30 | Ideal kite range |
| Wind 15–20 km/h | +10 | Light — larger kite |
| Wind 35–45 km/h | +10 | Strong — smaller kite |
| Wind > 45 km/h | -20 | Extreme — too dangerous |
| Wind < 15 km/h | -30 | Not enough wind |
| Gust factor < 1.3 | +10 | Steady |
| Gust factor > 1.6 | -10 | Gusty |
| Waves < 0.5m | +10 | Flat water |
| Waves < 1.5m | +5 | Moderate |
| Waves > 2.0m | -10 | Large |
| Cross-shore wind | +5 | Safest |
| Offshore wind | -5 | Dangerous |
| Top kite beach | +5 | Diani, Lamu, Kilifi |

### Kite Seasons on the Kenyan Coast

| Season | Months | Wind | Rating |
|--------|--------|------|--------|
| Kaskazi (NE monsoon) | Dec–Mar | 15–25 km/h NE | Good |
| Kusi (SE monsoon) | Jun–Sep | 20–35 km/h SE | Excellent |
| Transition | Apr–May, Oct–Nov | Variable | Fair |

### Best Kite Beaches
1. **Diani** — Flat lagoon behind reef, reliable wind
2. **Lamu (Shela)** — 12km of open beach, strong Kusi winds
3. **Kilifi** — Creek mouth creates flat water

## Swimming

### Ideal Conditions
- Waves: **< 0.5 m** (calm water)
- Wind: **< 20 km/h**
- No strong currents
- Daylight hours (7:00–17:00)
- No heavy rain

### Scoring Breakdown

| Factor | Points | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Effective waves < 0.3m | +20 | Very calm |
| Effective waves < 0.7m | +10 | Calm |
| Effective waves < 1.2m | -5 | Moderate |
| Effective waves > 1.2m | -25 | Rough |
| Wind < 15 km/h | +5 | Light |
| Wind > 30 km/h | -10 | Strong |
| Current > 0.5 m/s | -15 | Dangerous |
| Current-risk beach | -5 | Extra caution |
| Rain > 10mm | -10 | Uncomfortable |
| Daylight hours | +5 | Safe |
| After dark | -10 | Dangerous |

*Effective waves = actual waves × reef factor (0.4 for reef-protected)*

### Best Swimming Beaches
1. **Diani** — Reef lagoon is consistently calm
2. **Watamu** — Sheltered bays in marine park
3. **Shanzu** — Quiet, gentle shore

## Kids & Dogs

### Ideal Conditions
- Waves: **< 0.2 m** (very calm, paddling depth)
- Wind: **< 15 km/h** (comfortable)
- Tide: **low** (rock pools form, shallow areas)
- Time: **morning** (7:00–10:00, cooler)
- No currents, no rain

### Scoring Breakdown

| Factor | Points | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Effective waves < 0.2m | +25 | Perfect paddling |
| Effective waves < 0.5m | +10 | Gentle |
| Effective waves < 1.0m | -10 | Watch children |
| Effective waves > 1.0m | -30 | Not safe |
| Wind < 15 km/h | +10 | Comfortable |
| Wind > 25 km/h | -15 | Sandy, uncomfortable |
| Current < 0.2 m/s | +10 | Safe |
| Current > 0.3 m/s | -20 | Dangerous |
| Current-risk beach | -10 | Extra caution |
| Tide < 4.5m | +15 | Low tide — rock pools |
| Tide < 5.5m | +5 | Mid tide |
| Rising tide | -5 | Beach shrinking |
| Morning (7–10) | +10 | Cool, quiet |
| Afternoon (15–17) | +5 | Good light |
| Midday (11–14) | -5 | Hot sun |
| After dark | -15 | Not recommended |
| Rain > 5mm | -10 | Uncomfortable |
| Reef-protected | +10 | Calm lagoon |

*Effective waves = actual × 0.3 for reef-protected beaches*

### Why Low Tide is Best for Kids

On the Kenyan coast, low tide exposes:
- **Rock pools** teeming with small fish, crabs, and starfish
- **Wide sandy flats** for playing and building sandcastles
- **Shallow warm water** perfect for toddlers to splash in
- **Natural barriers** that prevent children from going too deep

The tidal range is 3–4 metres, so the difference between high and
low tide dramatically changes the beach character.

### Best Beaches for Kids
1. **Diani** — Reef lagoon = consistently calm, beautiful sand
2. **Watamu** — Marine park with shallow protected bays
3. **Shanzu** — Gentle, uncrowded shore
